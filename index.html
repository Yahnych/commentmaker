You can change the comment template (the text above) in any way you like. The stuff inside the square brackets are called the "keys", and let you assign custom values for each student. If you add new keys to the template, like [name] or [assignment], click the "Update Template" button. Your new keys will then be loaded above the template, and you can give them new values. After you've finished giving the keys new values, click the "Make Comment" button again to create your new comment. Read the instructions below for further details on how to completely customize the comment template in any way you like.

Instructions

Single words inside square brackets will be replaced with whatever words you give them in the template above. These are called the comment "keys". Here are some examples of comment keys.

[name]
[topic]
[assignment]

When you add these to your template, click the "Update Template" button and change their values. When you click "Make Comment", the keys will be replaced by the values you gave them.

Comment options are words in square brackets separated by commas. Here's an example of a comment option with three possible choices:

[excellent, good, fair]

The option chosen depends on the number you set for the student's ability level. For example, if you set the ability level to "2", then "good" will be chosen from the options above. Using this system you can quickly create many different comments from one template. It's a lot of fun to write, and you can use as many options as you like.

Finally, set the student's gender. This will determine whether the comment uses "he", "she", "him" or "her".

Remember: every time you change the comment template, click the "Update Template" button. Define your new comment keys and options, then click "Make Comment" to see your new comment. Keep experimenting until you find a combination of elements that you like.

The template is saved each time you click the "Make Comment" button, so there's no risk of losing your work.

Example template

Here's an example of a comment template to help you get started. Copy it into the template box above and customize it to make a template that's suitable for the course or subject that you're teaching.

[name] wrote [an excellent, a good, a fair] essay on [topic]. Her writing skills [are well-developed and, are developing and, need practice but] she wrote [a highly imaginative, thoughtful, acceptable] children's storybook. She has [an excellent, a very good, a reasonable] understanding of basic grammar, as exhibited by her quiz. [name] [produced work of a high standard and she has shown an impressive commitment in class, produced good quality work this term and [name]'s skills will continue to improve with practice, shows an ability in English but should remember to maintain focus when in class].
